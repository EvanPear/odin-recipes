Chicken Parmesan Recipe

How to Make Chicken Parmesan That's Never Soggy

Chicken Parmesan is a popular Italian-American dish that’s surprisingly easy to make at home. Many restaurant versions drown the chicken in too much sauce and cheese, making it soggy.

This method avoids that by putting only a little sauce on top of the chicken, keeping the breading crisp. Extra sauce can be served on the side if you prefer. This recipe uses a mix of mozzarella, Provolone, and real Parmesan for more flavor. Adding grated Parmesan to the breadcrumbs also enhances the taste.

Fry the breaded chicken until golden brown, then bake with sauce, cheese, and herbs. Simple and delicious!

Ingredients For Chicken Parmesan

  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup panko bread crumbs, or more as needed
  • 3/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese, divided
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour, or more if needed
  • 1 cup oil for frying, or as needed
  • 1 cup prepared tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup fresh mozzarella, cut into small cubes
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh basil
  • 1 cup grated provolone cheese
  • 4 teaspoons olive oil

Directions for Chicken Parmesan

  1. Gather the ingredients. Preheat an oven to 450 degrees F (230 degrees C).

  2. Place chicken breasts between two sheets of heavy plastic (resealable freezer bags work well) on a solid, level surface. Firmly pound chicken with the smooth side of a meat mallet to a thickness of 1/2-inch.

  3. Season chicken thoroughly with salt and pepper. Using a sifter or strainer; sprinkle flour over chicken breasts, evenly coating both sides

  4. Beat eggs in a shallow bowl and set aside. Mix bread crumbs and 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese in a separate bowl, set aside. Dip a flour-coated chicken breast in beaten eggs. Transfer breast to the bread crumb mixture, pressing crumbs into both sides. Repeat for each breast. Let chicken rest for 10 to 15 minutes.

  5. Transfer chicken to a baking dish. Top each breast with 2 tablespoons tomato sauce. Layer each chicken breast with equal amounts of mozzarella cheese, fresh basil, and provolone cheese. Sprinkle remaining Parmesan over top and drizzle each with 1/2 teaspoon olive oil.

  6. Bake in the preheated oven until cheese is browned and bubbly and chicken breasts are no longer pink in the center, 15 to 20 minutes. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least 165 degrees F (74 degrees C).
